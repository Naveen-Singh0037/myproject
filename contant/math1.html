Astronomy

INTRODUCTION

In career as astronomer, individuals are a type of scientist who studies primarily on space, which includes topics such as the stars, the planets, and galaxies. Astronomy is the science of planets, stars, galaxies, and the solar system. Would you like to learn about the universe and galaxies? Are you interested in understanding the why and how the universe works the way it does? If yes, then keep reading to know complete details on the career as an astronomer.

SKILLS REQUIRED

Enthusiasm & zeal to know well-organised approach to work self-motivation and focus patience and dedication keen interest in the astronomy

SCOPE

After completion of your degree in Astronomy, you can work as a research scientist with various research Institutions & big government organizations such as the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). You can also work as a researcher in the leading observatories and Institutions in the USA.

Undergraduate Courses:

  • Bachelor of Science in Astronomy.
  • Bachelor of Science in Liberal Arts and Sciences - Astronomy.
  • Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Astrophysics [Specialization]
  • BSc (Hons) Astronomy, Space Science and Astrophysics.

Master Courses :

  • Interdisciplinary Space Master
  • Physics and Astronomy - Gravitation, Astro- and Particle Physics
  • Particle Physics and Astrophysical Sciences
  • Physics and Astronomy - Biophysics and Biophotonics
  • Science (Astronomy)
  • Physics and Astronomy - Science for Energy and Sustainability

ELIGIBILITY

For pursuing a career in Theoretical Astronomy/observations, one can do B.Sc. in Physics and /or Mathematics after 10 + 2. Master’s level course in Astronomy with specialization in Theoretical Astronomy is offered by various institutes and universities. To pursue a career in Instrumentation/Experimental Astronomy, one can do Bachelor of Engineering (BE) in Electrical / Electronics/Electrical Communications, after 10 + 2. Then one can appear for the Joint Entrance Screening Test (JEST) examination, which makes one eligible to do specialization in astronomy leading to Ph. D. A professional astronomer also takes courses in microelectronics & instrumentation. One can also contact the Board of Graduate Studies at IIA bgs@iiap.ernet.in.

SALARY

Salary in the field of astronomy depends upon the profession you have chosen. If you choose your career in teaching the salary may be different from the one who are working as a researcher. The starting salary of an astronomer starts from Rs 50,000/- per month. Those who have earned experience and are highly qualified will earn an annual salary of Rs. 8 lacs to Rs 10 lacs.

RECOMMENDED ROADMAP

ENTRANCE EXAMS

Budget Analyst

INTRODUCTION

These are involved in policies governing the formulation and maintenance of the budget, but they also measure organisational performance, assess the effects of various programs and policies on the budget, and help draft budget-related legislation besides developing guidelines. Budget analysts work for government agencies, nonprofit charities, and private businesses.

SKILLS REQUIRED

Analytical skills: Budget analysts must be able to process a variety of information, evaluate costs and benefits, and solve complex problems. Communication skills: Budget analysts need strong communication skills because they often have to explain and defend their analyses and recommendations in meetings and legislative committee hearings. Detail oriented: Creating an efficient budget requires careful analysis of each budget item. Math skills: Most budget analysts need math skills and should be able to use certain software, including spreadsheets, database functions, and financial analysis programs. Writing skills: Budget analysts must present technical information in writing that is understandable for the intended audience.

SCOPE

● bachelor's in Accounting, Statistics or Finance (preferred) ● Certified Personal Accountant(preferred) ● Advanced Degree (preferred) ● Certified Government Financial Manager (preferred) ● A background in a budgetary roll ● Strong computational and mathematical abilities ● Robustinterpersonal skills - Must be a people person ● A good eye for detail ● Ability to pass a background check

Undergraduate Courses:

  • B.A [Economics].
  • B.Com.
  • BBA.
  • BSc Statistics.

Diploma Courses :

  • PGDM in Finance and Banking
  • PGP in Investment Banking and Capital Markets
  • PGP Banking and Finance

ELIGIBILITY

To become a budget analyst,the candidate must have a bachelor’s degree in Finance or business administration after that a master’s degree is mandatory as it helps you bag good job opportunities. However, with increasing competition, many companies also ask for certification. It helps boost your career prospects. Candidates can optfor certifications like Chartered Financial Analyst(CFA). Apartfrom the educational requirements, a budget analyst must have in-depth knowledge of mathematical, budgetary policies and superior analytical skills.

SALARY

The budget analystis offered great salary packages. With sufficient professional experience,they can easily fetch a six-figure salary. The starting salary of a budget analystin India is between Rs. 7,00,000 to Rs.10,00,000 and it increases with time and professional growth.
