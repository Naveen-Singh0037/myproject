Graphics Designing

INTRODUCTION

A graphic designer is a professional within the graphic design and graphic arts industry who assembles together images, typography, or motion graphics to create a piece of design. A graphic designer creates the graphics primarily for published, printed, or electronic media, such as brochures and advertising. They are also sometimes responsible for typesetting, illustration, user interfaces, and web design. A core responsibility of the designer's job is to present information in a way that is both accessible and memorable.

SKILLS REQUIRED

  • Creativity
  • Knowledge of Programming Languages
  • Visualisation Good Communication Skills
  • Ability to Work in a Team
  • Skill in visual design
  • Skill in visual design Good interpersonal skills
  • Knowledge of Colour theory

ELIGIBILTY

Aspirants who have cleared their 10+2 from any stream (Science/ Commerce/ Arts) can pursue a Graphic Design course at the graduation level. However, most popular colleges allow candidates to take part in their admission process only if they have completed their schooling from a recognised board.

SCOPE AND OPPORTUNITIES

In 2008, there were 2,86,100 people employed in graphic design jobs. By 2018, the U.S. Bureau of Labour Statistics predicts that the number of graphic design jobs will increase by 13%. The graphics designing industry is a very competitive industry when we speak in terms of freelancing (which is 25% of all graphic designers); with people all across the globe competing for projects. Even when we speak in terms of companies; there is a lot of competition between the firms to get the account. It is a very tough industry to compete in. Many inexperienced designers work long hours on assignments which cause many new comers in the field to become discouraged and leave the industry. Freelancing jobs are found on many websites online such as Corflot and Krop. Graphic design firms are located all over the world, mostly in large cities such as London, New York and Mumbai.

After pursuing a graphic design course aspirants are mostly hired by any of the below-mentioned firms:
  • Print & publishing houses (like newspapers, magazines, etc.)
  • Advertising agencies
  • Graphic design studios
  • TV & Multimedia production houses
  • Website development studios
  • E-learning companies
  • Web Design Studios
Popular job profiles in which aspirants are hired after pursuing a graphics design course are as follows:
  • Flash Animator

  • UI Designer
  • Graphic Designer

SALARY

A graphics designer in India who freelances earns approximately Rs. 90,000 to Rs. 525,000. A junior graphics designer has a salary of approximately Rs. 90,000 to Rs. 600,000 every year, while a senior graphics designer can earn from Rs. 180,000 to Rs. 800,000.

Cartoonist

INTRODUCTION

A Cartoonist is a professional who provides faces to the cartoon characters. Depending upon the area of interest a person can make a career as a Cartoonist in Newspapers, Comic Books or TV Channels. Some people draw cartoons on various issues such as social, religious, political and environmental. While some other sketch advertising cartoons or characters that entertain the masses. Most of the professionals in this field, draw cartoons to display and convey powerful messages which are of social importance.

SKILLS REQUIRED

  • Cartoon Drawing
  • Observant eye
  • Animation
  • Dialogue Writing
  • Creative
  • Drawing skill
  • Sense of humor
  • Language Skills
  • Storytelling

ELIGIBILITY

If a student aspires to become a cartoonist, then he/she can opt for humanities during 10+2, as the stream provides relevant subjects which will help the students in their career. Candidates from any stream can however pursue this course. The following are the eligibility criteria in becoming cartoonist in the USA
  • The candidates should obtain either an F Visa, M Visa or J visa.
  • The candidates pass the English proficiency test such as TOEFL, IELTS. (The minimum score required for the course is 70-80 for TOEFL and 6.5-7 for IELTS).
  • Some institutes require the candidates to pass the SAT exam.
  • The duration of the courses in the US is generally 4 years.
  • Candidates also need to produce their 12th or equivalent certificate at the time of admission.

SCOPE AND OPPORTUNITIES

With the introduction of technological advancements, animation and digital illustration has replaced traditional ways of drawing. There are a plethora of courses related to illustration and graphic design offered by top universities, colleges and even online platforms like Coursera and Udemy; based on personal interest and convenience, students choose the right course for themselves.

ENTRANCE EXAM

Exams such as NID DAT, ITM IDM etc. are gateways to undergraduate and postgraduate courses offered by various colleges in fine arts and animation.

SALARY

The salary of a cartoonist depends upon various factors; thus, it varies to a large extent. Earnings can be considerable and good if the characters gain popularity among the audience. Nowadays, you can pursue your career as a cartoonist by working on freelance projects. In India, the starting salary of a Cartoonist is around 1,80,000-2,50,000 INR p.a. and can extend up to 5,00,000-7,00,000 INR p.a. for senior-level positions.

RECOMMENDED ROADMAP

For pursuing a career as a cartoonist, you can opt for a bachelor’s or master’s degree in the fields of cartooning, animation, illustration, sequential arts, drawing etc. from top colleges/universities.

After the completion of 10+2, a candidate has four options available:

UG Preparation: Take a direct admission to an undergraduate course. Take admission after qualifying an entrance exam, pursue diploma or a certification course, apply for a job. The duration of undergraduate courses such as bachelor’s in fine arts, design and arts is 3 years. Apart from these courses the candidates can also look to upgrade their skill level by taking up various certificates courses in different software such as AutoDesk Sketchbook, Adobe Cartoon Animator Moho Debut, etc.,

PG Preparation: After the completion of a UG course, a candidate again has two options available. Pursue a postgraduate course in relevant field. Candidates can take the admission to different PG courses such as M.Des, MVA etc. Apply for a job

The duration of these courses is 2 years. A candidate can apply for jobs in newspapers, magazines, advertising and TV or film industry.

Candidates can also apply for the PhD courses that would help them to get lectureship at different colleges and pursue research in the relevant field.

After Qualifying Entrance Exam: Qualifying the entrance exam with a good rank is the beginning step in the career. After that, studying for consecutive 3 years to complete the undergraduate course a candidate has chosen. If interested, he/she can also pursue a postgraduate course in the relevant field, if the career demands it.

Start an Internship: After completion of the course the candidates should pursue an internship from a reputed company. The candidates can opt for the internship in their final year itself. The profile must be chosen in such a way so that it aligns with the preference of the candidates.
