CIVIL SERVICES

INTRODUCTION

The civil service is a collective term for a sector of government composed mainly of career civil servants hired on professional merit rather than appointed or elected, whose institutional tenure typically survives transitions of political leadership. A civil servant, also known as public servant, is a person employed in the public sector by a government department or agency for public sector undertakings. Civil servants work for central and state governments, and answer to the government, not a political party.

SKILLS REQUIRED

  • Civil Servants are expected to show understanding of national and international affairs, and trends
  • Good people skills
  • Capability to assess the mood of the people
  • Commitment to the governance of the nation
  • They need to have a good understanding of currents of thought, new discoveries, etc.
  • Good communication ability
  • Making sound decisions
  • Ability to take orders and maintain the line of command

SCOPE

This is a very reputative service. Government provides various facilities to its service workers. But, to achieve such post candidates have to pass UPSC exam but, this is second toughest exam in the world which judges candidates on many parameters. Getting a good post in civil service is the dream of many but, achieved by only few talented ones.

MAJOR CIVIL SERVICES

  • Indian Administrative Services (IAS)
  • Indian Foreign Services (IFS)
  • Bachelor of Arts in Archaeology and Museology
  • Indian Police Services (IPS)
  • Indian Revenue Services (IRS)
  • Indian Railway Traffic Services (IRTS)

ELIGIBILITY

  • You should hold a graduate bachelor’s degree in any subject (or about to take final year examinations) from a recognised institution.
  • You must be a minimum 21 years of age and maximum 32 years of age on the date of the examination (Relaxation of 5 years for SC/ST, individuals holding a domicile of Jammu & Kashmir; 3 years for OBC and defence personnel; 10 years for blind, deaf-mute and orthopedically handicapped persons.)
  • Individuals from General Category are allowed 6 attempts at taking the examination, OBC candidates are allowed 9 attempts, and SC/ST candidates have an unlimited number of attempts at the examination.
  • You should be a citizen of India to apply for the IAS and IPS, and for other deemed posts, an individual should be a citizen of India/Nepal/Bhutan/a Tibetan refugee who came over to India before 1st January, 1962/a person of Indian Origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka East African countries of Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, Ethiopia and Vietnam.

ENTRANCE EXAM AND PROCESS

It is conducted in three phases:

Stage I : Priliminary Examination-

Preliminary Examination consisting of General Studies Paper I and II (200 marks each) also popularly known as Civil Service Aptitude Test or CSAT. The question papers are objective (multiple choice questions) of two hours duration and are set in Hindi and English. English Language Comprehension skills is only in English

Stage II : Mains Examination -

It consists of nine papers in which two papers are qualifying and only marks of seven are counted. It is held in October every year. Results are announced in January.

  • Paper 1- One language (300 marks)
  • Paper 2 - English (300 marks)
  • Paper 3- Essay ( 200 marks)
  • Paper 4 & 5- General Studies (300 marks each)
  • Paper 6,7,8,9- Any 2 prescribed optional subjects(2 papers each subject) (300 marks for each paper)
Personality Test (interview) -

Candidates are invited after clearing Mains with cut-off marks. Selected candidates go through a two years probation period. Recruitment of Doctors, Engineers, Geologists, Economists and Statisticians for posts in the Central government organizations is carried out through separate examinations conducted by UPSC.It is held in March and final results are usually announced in May.

A successful candidate sits for 32 hours of examination during the complete process spanning around one year. sity and should have secured a minimum of 45%.

ARCHAEOLOGY

INTRODUCTION

Archaeology is the study of the ancient or recent human past through material remains. It is also a sub-discipline of Anthropology, which is the study of all human cultures. The experts are called as Archaeologist.

SKILLS REQUIRED

Enthusiasm & zeal to know with the developments in archaeology well-organised approach to work in team self-motivation and focus extra communication skills master in using various tools and instruments patience and dedication having good IT skills keen interest in the archaeology

SCOPE

This job is offered in government-owned museums for the safe upkeep and management of museum artefacts and also in private sectors. Once one gets a post-graduate diploma in archaeology, he/she can apply as a lecturer in various universities or institutions.

Aspirants can find job in government sectors through exam conducted by Union Public Service Commission or Staff Selection commission. A degree holder in Archaeology can also work as tourist guide, heritage mangers, interpreters’ resource persons of trip organisers in tourism.

Undergraduate Courses:

  • Diploma in Indian Archaeology
  • Bachelor of Arts in Indian History Culture and Archaeology
  • Bachelor of Arts in Archaeology and Museology

Master Courses :

  • MA in Archaeology
  • MA in Ancient Indian History and Archaeology
  • Master of Science in Archaeology
  • Master of Philosophy in Ancient Indian History, Culture and Archaeology
  • Post-Graduate Diploma in Archaeology
  • Doctor of Philosophy in Ancient Indian History, Culture and Archaeology

ELIGIBILITY

BA in Archaeology: The candidate needs to have done their class 12 from a recognised board and should have secured a minimum of 55%. The candidate can be from any stream. MA in Archaeology: The candidate needs to have done their graduation from a recognised university and should have secured a minimum of 45%. The candidate should also have done their BA in Archaeology/History/Museology or any similar field to be eligible.

SALARY

In India, as a fresher Archaeologist one can expect a salary between Rs.2.5 to 3.5 lakhs per year. Experienced and well-qualified professionals can expect salary packages between Rs.5 to 8 lakhs per annum. Salary packages will also depend upon education, working area, sector, level of skills, experience and many other factors. A doctoral degree is a must need for a better career and a good salary package in this field.

TOP INSTITUTES
