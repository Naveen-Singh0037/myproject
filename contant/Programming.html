Software Engineer

Introduction

A software engineer is a person who applies the principles of software engineering to design, develop, maintain, test, and evaluate computer software. The term programmer is sometimes used as a synonym, but may also lack connotations of engineering education or skills.

Scope

High

Competition

High

Eligibility

A minimum of 55% marks in 10+2 or equivalent is compulsory for pursuing B. Tech/B.E. in software engineering or diploma courses in the same. For admission in M. Tech in Software Engineering, candidates must have a passing certificate of graduation level with a minimum aggregate of 50% in the required field.

Income

Salary of a software engineer in India ranges from ₹ 3,00,000 to ₹ 50,00,000 lakhs per annum.

Recommended Roadmap

  • Decision Making: Students must search before pursuing the course. You must be prepared for pursuing the course to be a software developer. This is your life decision.
  • Subject Choices: Students must have to choose science as a stream in secondary school. You must choose physics, chemistry, maths (PCM) or physics, chemistry, maths, biology (PCMB) as the subject. You should be aware that you are interested in these subjects.
  • Entrance Exam Preparation: There are many entrance exams for a software developer like BITSAT, NATA, JEE Main, JEE advanced, SRMJEE. Prepare for the entrance exam after 10+2 standard. When you clear these entrance exams, you will get admission to a college or university for pursuing graduation.
  • Selecting Correct Colleges: Make a list of top colleges or universities which offer a course for software developers. You have to score in the entrance exam for admission to top colleges. Search about top colleges, faculties, fees, to make a good decision.
  • After Qualifying Exam: When you clear the entrance exam, your new journey of college life will start for a minimum of 4 years. 4 years will be full of theoretical and practical knowledge. Complete and participate in extracurricular activities to boost skills and experience. Start internships for enhancing skills.

Blogging

Introduction

Blogging is as simple as obtaining a website and publishing original content on it. Blogging refers to writing, photography, and other media that's self-published online. Blogging started as an opportunity for individuals to write diary-style entries, but it has since been incorporated into websites for many businesses. Tech-savvy bloggers can buy a domain name and build the website themselves. Those with less HTML knowledge can create an account with sites like WordPress that simplify the web design and publishing process.

Scope

High

Competition

High

Eligibility

Any one who is interested in content writing & possess a basic knowledge of websites & digital marketing can start.

Income

According to the data of Digital Uncovered, in India, a professional blogger can earn up to $10,000 every month. On average, a blogger can earn between $300 and $400 per month. Celebrity bloggers can earn in the range of $20,000 to $30,000 every month. Blogging has emerged as a serious profession for many people in India over the last few years.

Courses

Udemy, Coursera, Youtube, etc.

Recommended Roadmap

Start blogging as a side business. Slowly learn via online courses & implement those learnings on your blog. If possible, go and meet successful bloggers of your country & take guidance. When you'll start earning enough money from blogging, it's time to make it your full time job.

Perks

  • You can manage your online identity and build trust.
  • You will improve your writing skills.
  • You can get technical knowledge.
  • Showcase Your Professional Skills.
  • Earn Extra Money.
  • Market Your Business.
  • Land A Job Offer Fast.

Challenges

  • You May Invest Time, Sweat And Soul And Get Nothing In Return.
  • It Takes A Huge Upfront Investment Of Time And Work.
  • It’s A Lot More Difficult Than It Looks.

Freelance Programming

Introduction

Freelance programming involves contracting your coding and application development skills to various clients. Freelance programmers are self-employed, meaning they pay their own taxes, choose their own clients, set their own hours and manage their own workspace. As a freelance programmer, you can write code for websites, software, mobile apps and any other type of computing application. You communicate with clients to determine their needs, then complete the entire programming project from start to finish. Some programming professionals work on freelance projects part-time while still working at a full-time programming job. Others are independent contractors who sell their coding skills as their main source of income. Because freelance programmers have full control over the projects they accept, they can decide how many hours per week they want to dedicate to freelancing.

Scope

High

Competition

High

Eligibility

Knowledge of software development in at least one domain. Some popular domains are web development, iOS app development, Android app development and so on.

Income

Freelance Software Developer income in India ranges between ₹ 2.3 Lakhs to ₹ 20.0 Lakhs with an average annual income of ₹ 8.6 Lakhs.

Courses

You can do your learning by either going for formal training or opt for an ad hoc approach using online resources. There are plenty of online resources for almost all the domains out there, some of them are..
  • Popular Tutorial Websites like GeeksforGeeks, TutorialsPoint etc.
  • Online Forums like GeeksforGeeks Forum, StackOverflow, Quora etc.
  • Online course platforms like Edx, Udemy, Coursera, Udacity, Plural Sight, Khan Academy etc.
  • Major YouTube Channels like TheNewBoston, FreeCodeCamp.org, Envato Tuts+, mycodeschool, Derek Banas etc.

Recommended Roadmap

First of all, you will have to decide your domain, that is, the field of your interest/skill suitability. For example web development, iOS app development, Android app development and so on. You may just pick one and start targeted learning or you might like to explore a bit of everything before choosing a domain. You might already have expertise in one of the domains with experience of having some projects done. Whether you are an absolute beginner or an expert, the gaps in your understanding can be filled either by utilizing online resources like tutorials, documentation etc. or by undergoing a formal training. Each domain has its own skillset requirement. These skills include knowledge of programming languages and popular domain-specific frameworks. For example:
  • Java/Kotlin for Android App development.
  • HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for front end web development.
  • Python for App development on numerous platforms(like Django is a Python framework for web development) and many more.
  • In general, you will have to be very good with the use of suitable data structures and design and optimization of algorithms.
Once you are done with basics and have done a few projects, you can register yourself at some platform for freelancers and get started. There are many popular platforms like Freelancer, Upwork, Fiverr etc. It is advisable to start small and build your reputation. After building a good enough reputation and a brand name you can consider creating your own website for your work. This will increase your per-project income as well as further build up your brand.
